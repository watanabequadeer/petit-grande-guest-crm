Petit Grande — Guest data reality check Beds24 · internal

Source: Beds24 export, Petit Grande Miyabi — 9,176 stays, bookings Feb 2023–Jun 2026.

The core problem: you do not own your guest relationships Petit Grande runs almost entirely on Booking.com, and the data it returns is mostly anonymous. Of 9,176 stays, 65% have no real guest name and only 1.5% carry a usable email address. Today you cannot directly reach the guests you have already served — which is exactly why a guest CRM with first-party email capture is the highest-leverage project on the table.

1. Channel dependence

Booking.com alone drives 90.8% of all booking lines; OTAs combined are 95.9%. Direct (own site, desk, Google) is 4.1%.

Why this matters to the bottom line Booking.com commissions typically run ~15%. On near-total OTA dependence, that is a structural margin leak and a single point of failure — a rate or ranking change on one platform moves your whole revenue line.

Reality check: 12–15 person “target” segment Large 12–15 person groups are only 0.1% of history. The real base is couples and small families of 2–6. Pursuing very large groups is new-market acquisition, not activating the existing base — decide that deliberately.

4. Repeat behaviour

Among identifiable guests, only 7.6% booked more than once — largely a follow-up problem (no email, no re-marketing). Industry repeat benchmarks cited internally (45–70% for 2nd/3rd visits) show the size of the upside if owned contact and lifecycle email work.

Where the CRM pays for itself Capture consented emails at check-in, recognise returning guests, and run win-back plus seasonal offers. Repeat guests tend to book direct and sidestep OTA commission.
