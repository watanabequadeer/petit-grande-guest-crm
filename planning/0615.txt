What the meeting is really telling you
The notes cover a lot of ground (marketing channels, tour packaging, hiring drivers, CX tiers), but for this project — the folder is literally named GUEST CRM — the signal is clear. Three threads are tightly connected and form the actual buildable core:

The data foundation is broken. "Existing customer database exists but not being utilized" (line 23) and "begin collecting email addresses from all guests at check-in" (line 24). You can't do any of the marketing ambitions (newsletters, seasonal offers, repeat campaigns) until guest contact data is being captured reliably and centralized.
Repeat business is the real growth lever. The repeat-rate stat is the most important number in the whole doc: 20-30% → 45-50% → 70% across 1st/2nd/3rd visits (line 31). That means the highest ROI isn't new acquisition — it's a system that identifies returning guests and re-markets to them. CRM + email is exactly that engine.
Everything else is downstream. Tour packages, butler service, Reels content, Philippine drivers — these are real, but they're business/ops initiatives, not "GUEST CRM" deliverables. They become campaigns and segments inside the CRM once the foundation exists.
My recommended direction
Scope this project as a guest data + lifecycle marketing system, sequenced so value compounds:

Phase 1 — Capture & centralize. Standardize email/contact collection at check-in; migrate and clean the existing database into one source of truth.
Phase 2 — Segment by visit count. Implement the 1st/2nd/3rd-time guest tiering (the "tablecloth color-coding" idea) so repeat guests are visible and actionable.
Phase 3 — Lifecycle email. Welcome, post-stay feedback survey, seasonal offers, win-back — with open/click tracking (line 26).
Phase 4 — Feed the rest. Use the segments to power tour-package offers and the community/Reels funnel.
One flag: the notes say "Exploring CRM development using ClickUp" (line 22). ClickUp is fine as an internal ops board, but it is not a guest CRM or an email platform — pushing guest lifecycle marketing through it will fight you. That's a decision worth making deliberately rather than by default.
